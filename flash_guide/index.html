ðŸ”¹Follow these steps to flash TWRP, Custom ROM and Magisk on your Galaxy Tab A 10.1 (SM-T585):

    Device Preparation Before Starting

  • Step 1: Enable Developer Options by going to Settings then "About device" and tapping on "Build number" 7 times until the option is activated.
  • Step 2: Go back to the Settings menu and select "Developer options".
  • Step 3: Enable "USB Debugging" and "OEM Unlocking".

    • ðŸ”¹If you do not unlock the bootloader (step 3) before making any modifications to your device, you will see the message when you restart the device after the flashing process:

    Custom binary blocked by FRP lock.

    ðŸ”¹If you haven't followed the previous steps and this message appears, you will need to flash the stock firmware, for which you can find a tutorial link below. If you encounter any issues, you can join our Telegram group for assistance.

  • Step 4: Power down the device, then hold the Home, Power, and Volume Down buttons to enter Download mode.

    Setting Up Odin

  • Step 1: install Samsung USB drivers on .your PC.
  • Step 2: Download and launch the Odin application on your PC.
  • Step 3: Hook up the Samsung Galaxy Tab A to the PC using a USB cable.

    • ðŸ”¹Make sure the device is detected by Odin (you should see a message saying "added!!!").

    ðŸ”¹If the device isn't being recognised, double-check the USB connection and consider installing usb drivers on your PC.

  • Step 4: In Odin, click the AP button to find the 'TWRP file.tar' that you downloaded on your PC.
  • Step 5: Go to "Options" and uncheck the "Auto Reboot" box.

    Flashing TWRP

  • Step 1: Click "Start" in Odin to begin the flashing process.

    • ðŸ”¹A green box saying PASS! will appear when done.

  • Step 2: Hold down the Power button for a brief moment to turn off the device.

    • Immediately press and hold the Volume Up, Home, and Power buttons simultaneously until the Samsung logo appears on the screen.

    ðŸ”¹Note: TWRP Recovery asks if you'd like to use it in 'Read Only' mode. 'Read Only' mode means it will remain on your device without making changes until you reboot. Simply swipe the arrow to allow modifications.

    In TWRP Recovery Mode

  • Step 1: Select 'Wipe', then 'Format data', and confirm with 'yes'.
  • Step 2: Go back to TWRP home, select 'Reboot', then 'Recovery'.

    • ðŸ”¹If you don't want to install a custom ROM and prefer to stay on the stock ROM, you can stop at this step and choose "Reboot," then "System." Alternatively, you can proceed to the "Flashing Magisk" section if you want to root your device.

    Flashing the Custom ROM

  • Step 1: Repeat the 'Wipe' process, but this time select 'Advanced Wipe', then select all options (except SD Card and USB OTG).
  • Step 2: Select Storage (if your custom ROM is on SD Card or USB OTG).
  • Step 3: Select the zip file of your custom ROM.
  • Step 4: Click on 'Add More Zips' and select the GApps file included with the ROM if it requires it.
  • Step 5: Swipe the arrow to confirm the flash and wait until the flashing process is complete, then press 'reboot system'.

    • ðŸ”¹Note: In the Android 10 ROMs section, you will find a port for One UI 2.0, along with a file named 'create vendor.' You need to flash this file, then perform a reboot to recovery, and finally flash the port.

    ðŸ”¹Note: If you install a custom ROM on your device and wish to switch to another custom ROM, you must perform the Advanced Wipe step again (Step 1) first, and then flash the ROM you wish to switch to.

    ðŸ”¹Note: If you install any custom ROM on your device and later wish to flash the One UI 1 port available in the Android 9 ROMs section, you must first flash the stock firmware of the device and then repeat the previous steps again.

    ðŸ”¹Note: A "dirty flash" refers to the process of flashing a new version of your ROM over the top of the one you have, without using the "Wipe Data/Advanced Wipe/Factory Reset" option in recovery. Even if you intend to dirty flash the update, you should always wipe cache and dalvik cache.

  • Step 4: Wait until the flashing process is complete, then press 'reboot system'.
  • Step 5: After rebooting the system, install the Magisk application on your device.
